# MarketPulse: A Systematic Momentum & Relative Strength Screener

> **You Don’t Need Better Predictions. You Need a Better Framework.**

## The Problem: Framework Failure, Not Market Failure
One of the most persistent issues in modern investing is not a lack of information, but a lack of framework. Many investors describe themselves as long-term, yet their behaviour tells a different story—struggling through volatility, taking gains prematurely, or losing discipline when returns compound slowly. 

This is often reinforced by the belief that success comes from being smarter than the market, shifting focus toward prediction rather than process. The result is a clear disconnect between stated intent and actual behaviour—a framework failure, not a market failure.

The underlying issue is that analysis often remains static while the market continuously evolves, causing decisions to be shaped by past outcomes rather than current risk. Since the post-COVID period, markets have become more dynamic, with faster leadership rotation and wider dispersion, making static portfolios less effective. Indexes such as the S&P 500 and Nasdaq illustrate why. Although labelled “passive,” they adapt through systematic reconstitution and rebalancing, preserving market-level exposure as leadership changes. Most individual investors fail to replicate this not because they lack ideas, but because they resist rebalancing.

## The Thesis: All Investment Paths Lead to Momentum
This investment thesis is centred on a clear framework that works across different market conditions. Inspired by index investing, it focuses on identifying the prevailing market regime and aligning exposure to momentum, recognising that markets continuously evolve as risk shifts and leadership changes. 

At its core, all investment styles depend on the same outcome: **price appreciation.** Investors are rewarded not simply when a company improves its business, but when that improvement is reflected in the stock price. Value, growth, and technical approaches differ in how opportunities are identified—earnings momentum, business acceleration, or price trends—but the underlying mechanism is the same. **Without momentum, returns cannot be realised.**



## The Framework: Four Core Decision Variables
The framework is structured around four core decision variables:

* **When to Buy:** Conditioned on market regime and trend stability, avoiding exposure during unfavourable environments.
* **What to Buy:** Determined by the presence of momentum and relative strength. Capital is allocated only to assets already being rewarded by the market.
* **How Much to Buy:** Guided by risk, ensuring position sizes remain appropriate as volatility changes.
* **When to Sell:** Driven by deterioration in momentum or systematic rebalancing requirements—not by emotion or prediction.

## Portfolio Construction & Execution
The model translates this framework into practical portfolio decisions. Stock selection is driven by momentum and relative strength, ensuring capital is allocated only to assets already being rewarded by the market. Rather than predicting future winners, the focus is on identifying stocks where positive price behaviour is persistent and confirmed.

* **Risk-Aware Filtering:** To manage behavioural and tail risk, stocks exhibiting extreme greed or overcrowded conditions are filtered out. This helps avoid late-stage momentum where upside becomes increasingly asymmetric to downside. The intent is not to chase strength blindly, but to participate where momentum exists without excessive speculative risk. 
* **Equal Weighting:** Portfolio construction remains intentionally simple. The model selects 20 stocks with equal weighting, reducing concentration risk and preventing any single position from dominating portfolio outcomes.
* **Systematic Timing (Regime Filtering):** Rather than remaining invested at all times, the model first evaluates whether market conditions support sustained momentum. When the environment deteriorates and uncertainty rises, trends tend to break down quickly, making momentum less reliable. In these phases, exposure is reduced or avoided, recognising that broad market pressure often overwhelms individual stock strength.
* **Regular Rebalancing:** Anchored in index-style discipline, the framework is reviewed and rebalanced at least quarterly to realign weights as relative performance, volatility, and correlations evolve.

### Process Over Prediction
By combining momentum-based selection, risk-aware filtering, equal-weight portfolio construction, and market regime controls, the model aims to allocate capital where expected returns are statistically more favourable while limiting exposure when risk is poorly compensated. 

The objective is not to predict outcomes, but to maintain consistent alignment between portfolio risk and prevailing market dynamics through structured, repeatable execution.
